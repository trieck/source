The magic-user class is an exceedingly powerful profession. The arcane
powers of the realm of magic is at their beck and call, and woe to those who
show little (or not enough) respect to the magic-user! Magic-users require
high intelligence to learn and master these difficult powers. Magic-users
must have a minimum of 9 in intelligence; however, a high score is required
should the character wish to be able to use the most powerful magics.
Magic-user with an intelligence of 16 or greater gain a 10% experience
bonus.

Magic-users have little in the way of combat skills, and are the weakest
class in combat. They cannot wear armor, and have a very limited selection
of weapons to chose from. However, they compensate for this lack of close
combat aptitude by the ability to use a wide array of wands, staves, and
other powerful magic items.

Due to the wide variety of type of magical spells, most magic-users gain
only a general knowledge of all the areas. Some magic-users choose to
specialize, and concentrate on one area of magic. These specialists gain
bonuses when using magic in their area of choice, but suffer penalties when
using other areas, as they have neglected them to specialize. See below for
more details in magic specialization.

When a magic-user reaches 12th level, construction of magic items, scrolls,
and potions is possible. This tends to be a time-consuming and costly
process, so most magic-users seldom make more than a few items in their
careers. However, it is possible to create vast and powerful magics as
legends speak of, albeit with lots of money, a great deal of time, and not a
little luck!

Upon reaching 12th level, magic-users may build a tower and clear an area of
10 to 20 mile radius around it. They may collect revenues of 5sp per
inhabitant from their subjects.

Specialization

Magic-users may chose to specialize in one of three ways: school magic,
elemental magic, or wild magic. Originally, magic was divided into schools,
according to the effects of the spell. The 9 schools are: Abjuration,
Alteration, Conjuration/Summoning, Greater Divination, Lesser Divination
(Divinations spells under 4th level), Enchantment/ Charm, Illusion,
Invocation/Evocation, and Necromancy. Elemental magic came about as an
alternative method, where spells were classified according to the raw
element they used: Fire, Water, Earth, or Air. Recently, a new field of
magic has been discovered: Wild Magic. Wild magic spells deal with great
amounts of free flowing energy, which can produce spectacular results, but
often with unpredictable side-effects. Each specialist field has advantages
and drawbacks.

School Magic

A specialist in school magic must choose a field to concentrate in (not
including Lesser Divination). A specialist gains one additional spell at
each spell level if it is in their specialty. That is, a 3rd level
specialists may memorize one additional spell of both 1st and 2nd level.
Specialists may not use spells or magic items of opposition schools.
Opposition schools are listed below. Ex. Alteration and Illusion are
opposition schools to Abjuration.
[Image]

Specialists gain a bonus of +1 to save vs. their specialty magic, and -1 on
targets' saves vs. their specialty magic. They add +15% to their chance to
learn a spell of their specialty, but subtract -15% when attempting to learn
spells outside their specialty. In addition to a minimum intelligence of 15,
specialists have other minimum ability scores, which vary according to which
specialty they choose.

Elemental Magic

Instead of dividing magic into schools according to the purpose of the
magic, elementalists choose to separate magic according to which of the four
basic elements they use. Naturally, some spells use no discernable elements;
these are considered "other" by elementalists. Similar to school
specialists, elementalists choose a element to specialize in. They may not
cast spells (or use magic items) of the opposite element. Elementalists gain
a +25% bonus when trying to learn a spell in their specialty element, a +15%
bonus when trying to learn a spell of another element, but suffer a -25%
penalty when attempting to learn non-elemental spells. Elementalists gain +2
on their saves vs. magic of own element, -2 on the target's save. One
additional spell per spell level is also gained, provided that the
additional spell is in the elementalist's specialty. Additionally,
elementalists may cast 1 spell per day at 1-4 levels higher for duration/etc
purposes. At 15th level, an elementalist does not have to concentrate to
control a summoned elemental of his specialty. At 20th level, there is no
chance of summoned elemental turning on the wizard.

Wild Magic

Wild Magic is a new area of study. Wild mages deal with magic that is only
barely under control - often times, the result is not exactly what was
expected, and sometimes a truly bizarre effect occurs. Like other
specialists, wild mages gain 1 additional spell per spell level bonus, a
+10% bonus when attempting to learn wild magic, but a -5% penalty when
attempting to learn all others. Wild magic has no opposition school.

10th Level Spells

10th level spells are extremely powerful, rare, and difficult magics to
obtain and use. The use of 10th level spells is restricted to certain
wizards, and may only be cast under certain circumstances. To use 10th level
magic, a wizard must have a minimum of 19 Intelligence and be of 25th level.
For a full explanation of the gaining, use, and special circumstances
surrounding 10th level magic, see the Chapter on Magic in the part on
Campaigns.
