Notes:

1. The cavalier and paladin have a hit point range of 4-13 points at 1st
level, generated either by 3d4+1 or d10+3 depending upon the social
standing of the character at the start of his or her career. cf.
Cavalier
2. Starting at 9th level, the paladin gains clerical spell ability, and
may eventually cast spells of up to 4th level.
3. The ranger begins with two eight-sided hit dice (d8) and thereafter
goes up one hit die per level.
4. Starting at 8th level, the ranger gains druidic spell ability, and may
eventually cast spells of up to 3rd level.
5. Thieves of 10th level or higher and assassins of 12th level or higher
gain the ability to read magic-user spells from scrolls.
6. The monk begins with two four-sided hit dice (d4) and thereafter goes
up one hit die per level.

Notes:

1. A cavalier or paladin will not wear leather, padded, or studded leather
armor,because such garb is beneath the character's station. cf.
Cavalier
2. Certain deities may restrict the type of armor worn; the DM should feel
free to customize this category as circumstances warrant.
3. A barbarian can wear any sort of armor, but does not gain the full
benefit of the dexterity bonus of the class if the armor worn is of the
bulky or fairly bulky variety.
4. A thief attired in anything other than leather armor must take certain
adjustments to the chance of success in performing certain thieving
functions. See Table~\ref{armor-effects-thief}.

Notes:

1. A cavalier or paladin will not use pole arms, missile weapons, or other
types of arms that are commonly associated with the lower social
classes. cf. Cavalier
2. The use of oil in personal combat is unacceptable to the cavalier and
paladin, but not in siege warfare.
3. Certain deities may allow their clerics and/or sohei to use additional
weapons and/or forbid the use of listed ones. The list is generalized
for a wide variety of deities; the DM should feel free to modify it as
conditions warrant.
4. Istari prefer one-handed weapons, as they allow for spell-casting more
easily. Staffs are also preferred.
